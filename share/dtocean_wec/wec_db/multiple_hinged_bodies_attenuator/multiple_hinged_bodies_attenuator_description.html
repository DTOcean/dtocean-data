Pelamis Attenuator

General Description

The device used in this example consists of 5 jointed aligned cylinders and is allowed to move in surge, heave and pitch.

Besides, generalized degrees of freedom, pitching around the 4 joint connections between consecutive cylinders, are taken

into account. Overall, the device motion is modelled through these 3+4 independent modes of motion.

Geometry

All cylindrical sections have the exact same geometry of 2 m radius and 36 m length, which leads to an overall length

of the device of 180 m. The mesh of the submerged part of an intermediate cylinder is shown in the figure below.

The mesh is composed of 290 panels but for the heading and the tailing cylinders, which are modelled with 320 panels,

with the extra 30 panels coming from the heading and tailing submerged circular covers closing the device.

Body description

The geometric centre of the device is taken as the (0, 0, 0) of the global reference system. The heading cylinder

is taken as the first body of the chain and having no parents at all, and the following cylinder as its child. Then,

consecutive cylinders are parents of their following and children of the former, so that the tailing cylinder, the last

body in the chain, has no children. The relative degree of freedom between parents and children is a rotation around the

y-axis.

Mass

The mass of each cylinder is 1350000 kg, while the inertia tensor is assumed:

[147150000.0, 0.0, 0.0;

0.0, 147150000.0, 0.0;

0.0, 0.0, 147150000.0] kg m^2.

PTO

The PTO is associated to the generalized pitch modes and the damping coefficient is assumed to be 250000000 N m s/rad for

all of them.

Mooring

No mooring stiffness is taken into account.
